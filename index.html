Women's Safety,Everyone’s Responsibility!

From 2000 to 2025, incidents of violence and rape against women in Bangladesh have increased, creating deep concern in society. To address this issue, it is our collective responsibility to raise awareness, show respect towards women, and ensure swift justice. Ensuring women's safety is the shared responsibility of all of us.

What We Do

Strengthening Laws

Stronger Punishments: Implement stricter laws, including fast-track trials and life imprisonment for rapists. Better Law Enforcement: Train police officers on handling sexual violence cases sensitively.

Empowerment

Economic Empowerment: Support women’s entrepreneurship, equal pay, and access to jobs. Educational Empowerment: Provide quality education, scholarships, and skills training. Health & Safety: Ensure access to reproductive health services, mental health support, and safe spaces.

Media and Online Safety

Regulating Online Harassment: Implement strict laws against cyberbullying and online sexual harassment. Stop Misrepresentation in Media: Ensure that movies, dramas, and advertisements promote gender equality instead of objectifying women.

Use of Technology

Surveillance and technology in public spaces: Increase the use of surveillance technology (like CCTV) in public spaces, transit, and areas with high foot traffic. Safety apps: Encourage the development of mobile apps where women can quickly send alerts to their families or authorities in case of an emergency.

School and College Education Programs

Self-Defense Training: Schools and colleges should provide self-defense classes for girls. Strict Action Against Harassment: Schools and universities should have strict anti-harassment policies.

Victim Support Services

Establish support centers: Create more women’s shelters, counseling services, Psychological and legal aid to support survivors of sexual violence. Free medical care: Provide immediate free medical care for survivors of sexual assault, including forensic evidence collection.
